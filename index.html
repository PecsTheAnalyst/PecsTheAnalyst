Project 01 / SQL → Power BI

Chocolate Sales
End-to-End Analytics

The Challenge
Squillion, a global chocolate company, needed a single place to monitor $43M+ in sales across 6 countries, 3 teams, and 20 salespeople — and understand what was driving performance.
What I Built
A full SQL-to-Power BI pipeline: wrote SQL views to pre-process the raw data, then built a 3-page interactive dashboard covering overall sales, product trends, and individual salesperson performance.
$43.6M Total Revenue
6 Countries
7.6K Orders
Key Finding
Wilone O'Kielt generates the highest revenue per sale ($6.2K avg) while Bars account for nearly half of all revenue. December is peak season — a clear signal for inventory planning.

How I Work

From raw data
to real decisions.

01
Understand the Business

Before touching a dataset, I ask: what decision needs to be made? What does "success" look like? Every analysis starts with a clear business question.

02
Clean & Prepare the Data

Real data is messy. I use SQL, Power Query, and Python to clean, reshape, and structure data so it's reliable enough to trust — and fast enough to query.

03
Find the Story

Numbers are only useful if they tell you something. I dig for the pattern, the outlier, the trend that changes what a business does next.

04
Communicate Clearly

A great insight locked inside a spreadsheet is useless. I build dashboards and reports that non-technical stakeholders can read, understand, and act on immediately.
